How it works

  1. You submit the form (EN/ES) – share the URLs you want removed, platforms involved and a short description.
  2. We verify ownership and collect evidence – we check that you own or represent the content and save screenshots / records platforms usually require.
  3. We file takedown / abuse notices – DMCA or policy-based reports in English or Spanish, usually within 24–48h on business days.
  4. We follow up – we send reminders or escalations after ~72h if the content is still live.
  5. You get a summary – which links were removed, which are pending and which were escalated.

FAQ

What kind of cases do you handle?
Mainly stolen or leaked photos/videos, copyright violations, impersonation profiles and harmful or abusive posts. If you’re not sure, send us the links and we’ll review for free.

Which platforms do you cover?
We regularly file reports to Reddit, X (Twitter), Instagram, TikTok, YouTube, OnlyFans, Fansly and major hosting providers. For other sites we’ll check their abuse / legal contacts and advise what’s realistic.

How long does removal take?
First actions usually happen within 24–48h after you submit the form. Actual removal depends on the platform – some respond within hours, others may take a few days.

Do you guarantee 100% removal?
No one can honestly guarantee 100%. We focus on cases with a strong policy or legal basis, and we only charge the success fee for confirmed removals.

Will my real name be visible?
Where possible, we file notices as your representative. For some copyright or legal notices platforms may require your legal name – in those cases we explain exactly what will be shared before we send anything.
